3. **Figure References/Titles**: Identifiers for figures, diagrams, illustrations
   - **Label patterns**: "Figure", "Fig", "Diagram", "Chart", "Illustration", numbered variations, etc.
   - **Separators**: May use em-dash (–), hyphen (-), colon (:), or other separators
   - **Context clues**: May be captions, standalone titles, or references within lists

4. **Equation Titles/References**: Identifiers and titles for mathematical expressions or formulas
   - **Reference patterns**: "Equation", "Eq", "Formula", "Form", numbered variations, etc.
   - **Numbering formats**: (5.1), (A-3), (2-1), 5.1, A.3, etc.
   - **Layout patterns**:
     * Separate line with title: "Equation 5.1: Complex equation" (title to the right of number)

     * Inline with number: "x = expression (6-11)" or "(2-5) F = ma"
   - **Title positioning**: When present, titles appear to the right of the equation number
   - **No title cases**: Many equations have only numbers without descriptive titles - leave title blank
   - **Focus**: Identify equation REFERENCES and TITLES, not the mathematical expressions themselves
   - **Use your knowledge**: Apply standard mathematical document conventions

5. **Table of Contents/Navigation Entries**: Navigation elements listing document content
   - **Unified purpose**: All serve as navigation/reference to other parts of document
   - **Common characteristics**: Sequential listing of document sections or elements in the order they appear in the document
   - **Individual line format examples**:
     * "1.1 Introduction .................... 5"
     * "Chapter 2: Methodology"
     * "Table 5-1 – Operation precedence .... 19"
     * "Figure 9-11 – Flowchart 107"
     * "Appendix A"
     * "Bibliography ...................... 245"
     * etc.
   - **Pattern variations**: May include page numbers (right-aligned, inline, or absent), leader characters (dots, dashes, or none), various numbering schemes
   - **Context**: Multiple consecutive entries with similar formatting pattern, organized in the sequence they appear in the document
   - **Key distinction**: These REFERENCE content, they are not the content itself
   - **Use your knowledge**: Apply your understanding of table of contents conventions across different document types and formatting styles

**Important Context Guidelines**:
- Use your knowledge of diverse document formatting conventions
